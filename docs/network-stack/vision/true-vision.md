## The Trinity of Decentralization

### Bitcoin (2009): Decentralized Money
**Satoshi's Vision:**
> "A purely peer-to-peer version of electronic cash would allow online payments to be sent directly from one party to another without going through a financial institution."

**Key Principles:**
- No central authority
- Cryptographic proof, not trust
- Peer-to-peer transactions
- Pseudonymous (not tied to real identity)
- Be your own bank

**What Happened:**
- тЬЕ Technology succeeded (blockchain works!)
- тЭМ Vision failed (centralized exchanges, KYC, speculation)
- тЭМ Banks won (Coinbase = bank with extra steps)
- тЭМ Governments won (KYC/AML on every exchange)
- тЭМ Users lost (fees, surveillance, no privacy)

**Why It Failed:**
- Fiat on-ramps require KYC (government pressure)
- Exchanges became banks (centralization creeps back)
- Scalability issues (Lightning = trusted hubs)
- Became investment, not currency (HODLing culture)

---

### Plan 9 (1992): Distributed Computing
**Bell Labs Vision:**
> "Your CPU can be in California, your storage in New York, your display in Tokyo. The network makes it all one system."

**Key Principles:**
- Everything is a file
- Network transparency
- Distributed by design
- Simple, elegant, composable
- No central servers

**What Happened:**
- тЬЕ Technology succeeded (9P protocol, ideas spread)
- тЭМ Adoption failed (too radical for corporate IT)
- тЭМ Ideas stolen (cloud companies = Plan 9 + surveillance)
- тЭМ Users lost (AWS/Google = Plan 9 philosophy, centralized control)

**Why It Failed:**
- Too different from Unix (scared users away)
- No killer app (technically superior тЙа market success)
- Poor timing (1992 networks too slow)
- No commercial backing (AT&T breakup)

---

### Napster (1999): Peer-to-Peer Sharing
**Shawn Fanning's Vision:**
> "Your music collection is my music collection. Share everything with everyone. No central storage, just a community of peers."

**Key Principles:**
- Peer-to-peer file sharing
- No central storage (just index)
- Everyone contributes
- Community collaboration
- Information wants to be free

**What Happened:**
- тЬЕ Technology succeeded (P2P works!)
- тЭМ Legally destroyed (RIAA lawsuit, 2001)
- тЭМ Replaced by centralization (iTunes, Spotify)
- тЭМ Users lost (subscriptions instead of ownership)
- тЭМ Sharing became "piracy" (criminalized)

**Why It Failed:**
- Central index = single point of failure (legal target)
- Record labels sued it to death
- Replaced with "legal" alternatives (DRM, streaming)
- Users traded freedom for convenience
